# Case Study: Customer Segmentation Using Clustering

## Problem Statement

An e-commerce company wants to better understand its customers to improve marketing strategies and increase sales.

However, the company does not know which customers belong to premium, regular, or low-value categories.

---

# Available Data

The company collects the following information:

* Customer Age
* Gender
* Annual Income
* Purchase Frequency
* Average Order Value
* Total Spending
* Website Visit Frequency

---

# Why Are Labels Unavailable?

The dataset does not contain predefined categories such as:

* Premium Customer
* Regular Customer
* Discount Shopper

Since these labels do not exist, supervised learning cannot be applied directly.

---

# How Clustering Helps

K-Means Clustering can automatically group customers with similar purchasing behavior.

Example clusters:

## Cluster 1

Premium Customers

Characteristics:

* High Income
* High Spending
* Frequent Purchases

---

## Cluster 2

Regular Customers

Characteristics:

* Moderate Income
* Consistent Purchases

---

## Cluster 3

Discount-Oriented Customers

Characteristics:

* Lower Spending
* Purchase During Sales

---

# Insights Generated

The business can identify:

* High-value customers
* Loyal customers
* Occasional shoppers
* Price-sensitive customers

---

# Business Benefits

## Personalized Marketing

Different campaigns can be created for each customer group.

## Improved Customer Retention

Special rewards can be offered to loyal customers.

## Better Product Recommendations

Products can be recommended based on cluster behavior.

## Increased Revenue

Targeted marketing improves conversion rates and sales.

---

# Conclusion

Customer segmentation using clustering enables organizations to understand customer behavior without requiring labeled data. These insights help businesses optimize marketing strategies, improve customer satisfaction, and increase profitability.
