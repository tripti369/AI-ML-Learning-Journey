# Case Study: House Price Prediction

## What is the model trying to predict?

The model is trying to predict the selling price of a house based on features such as:

* Area
* Number of bedrooms
* Location
* Age of the house

---

## Example of a Correct Prediction

Actual Price = ₹50,00,000

Predicted Price = ₹49,80,000

The prediction is very close to the actual value, so the model performed well.

---

## Example of an Incorrect Prediction

Actual Price = ₹50,00,000

Predicted Price = ₹35,00,000

The prediction is far from the actual value, resulting in a large error.

---

## What Kind of Error Can Occur?

The model may:

* Predict a price that is too high
* Predict a price that is too low

Both situations create prediction errors.

---

## Why Is Reducing Error Important?

* Improves prediction accuracy
* Increases trust in the model
* Helps buyers and sellers make better decisions

---

## How Does Repeated Training Improve the Model?

1. The model studies historical house data.
2. It makes predictions.
3. The loss function calculates errors.
4. The model adjusts its parameters.
5. Training repeats over multiple epochs.

As training continues, errors decrease and predictions become more accurate.
